When a tech submits a service estimate of $100 or more, it waits for a decision. (Estimates under $100 auto-approve on submit.) You make that decision from the service ticket's detail page while it's in the **Awaiting Approval** state.

## The three actions

- **Approve Estimate** — then enter a note: *"Who told us to approve?"* (required — e.g., "Spoke with John Smith 4/29, approved verbally"), and tap **Confirm Approve**. The ticket moves to **Approved** and work can start.
- **Decline** — enter *"Why are we declining?"* (required for the record), then **Confirm Decline**.
- **Request More Info** *(managers only)* — opens a box for *"What do you need from the tech?"*; tap **Send Back to Tech**. The ticket returns to the tech with your note so they can revise the estimate.

## Notes

- The approve/decline note is required and is saved to the ticket's history, so there's always a record of who authorized the call.
- The customer can also approve the estimate themselves through the emailed approval link — see [Create a service ticket and send an estimate](/help/office/create-a-service-ticket). When they sign off there, the ticket flips to **Approved** without you needing to act.
