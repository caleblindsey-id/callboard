This is the core of a technician's day: opening a preventive-maintenance ticket, doing the work, and recording it so it can be billed. CallBoard saves your progress as you go, so you won't lose anything if you get interrupted.

## Before you start

- You'll need the machine in front of you to read its **hour meter** and **date code**.
- You'll capture the **customer's signature** at the end, so do this while you're still on site.

## Step 1 — Open the ticket and start work

1. From your **Dashboard** or **My PMs**, tap the ticket you're working.
2. Review the customer, location, and equipment at the top.
3. Tap **Start Work**. The ticket moves to *In Progress* and the completion form appears.

> If the PM can't be done today (machine down, customer reschedule), tap **Request Skip** instead, enter a reason, and submit. A manager reviews skip requests.

## Step 2 — Fill out the work order

Work down the form. Required fields are marked with a red asterisk (\*).

- **Completion Date** — defaults to today; change it if you did the work earlier.
- **Hours Worked** — your labor time on the PM.
- **Machine Hours \*** — the reading from the equipment's hour meter (e.g. `1247.5`).
- **Date Code \*** — the stamped date/lot code on the machine (e.g. `26W15`).

### PM Service (blue section)

This is the work **covered under the maintenance agreement**. The parts that come standard for this machine are already filled in for you. You don't see or enter pricing here — the PM is billed at its flat rate.

### Additional Work (amber section)

Use this **only** for work *beyond* the agreement that the customer should be charged for:

- **Additional Labor Hours** — extra time beyond the PM, billed at the shop labor rate.
- **Additional Parts** — search for and add any parts used. Pricing **is** shown here because it's billable.

As you add additional work, a running **Grand Total** appears at the bottom (PM flat rate + any additional work). Taxes are not included in this total.

### Completion Notes

Note anything the office or customer should know about the visit.

## Step 3 — Photos and (if needed) ACE labor

- **Service Photos** — tap **+ Add Photo** to attach pictures of the work. Photos are compressed automatically, so don't worry about file size.
- **+ Add ACE Labor** — only if you did extra no-charge labor that should still count toward your payout. Enter the hours and a reason for your manager. This does **not** appear on the customer's invoice. Skip this if it doesn't apply.

## Step 4 — Signature and submit

1. Have the customer sign in the **signature box**, and type their **printed name**. Both are required.
2. Tap **Mark Complete**.

CallBoard checks a few things before it accepts the ticket:

- Machine Hours, Date Code, and the customer signature + printed name must be filled in.
- **All ordered parts must be marked received.** If a part is still on order, you'll see a message and can't complete yet — the office needs to receive the part in the [Parts Queue](/help/office/order-parts) first.

If everything checks out, the ticket moves to *Completed* and the next PM for this machine is scheduled automatically.

## Saving as you go

You don't have to finish in one sitting. CallBoard **auto-saves your progress** a few seconds after each change, and you can tap **Save Progress** any time. Close the app and come back — your work will be waiting.

## After you submit

From the completed ticket you can generate the **work order PDF** and share it with the customer (tap to prepare it, then share or download). The office takes it from there for billing.

## Common questions

- **Why can't I complete the ticket?** Almost always an unreceived part, a missing Machine Hours / Date Code, or a missing signature. The error message at the top tells you which.
- **I put a part in the wrong section.** Remove it and re-add it in the correct one — PM Service for agreement-covered parts, Additional Work for billable extras.
- **I made a mistake after completing.** Ask a manager — they can reopen the ticket.
