Sometimes a scheduled PM can't be done — the machine is down, the customer wants to reschedule, or access isn't available. You don't just leave it; you **request a skip** so a manager can review and decide.

## How to request a skip

1. Open the PM ticket from your **Dashboard** or **My PMs**.
2. Tap **Request Skip**. (It's available before you start the work, and also inside the completion form if you've already tapped Start Work.)
3. In **"Why should this PM be skipped?"**, give a clear reason — for example, "Customer requested to reschedule" or "Machine is down, parts on order."
4. Tap **Submit Skip Request**.

The ticket moves to **Skip Requested** and waits for a manager. You'll see a note that your request is pending approval.

## What happens next

A manager reviews the reason and either:

- **Approves it** — the PM is skipped and the schedule moves on to the next service date, or
- **Denies it** — the ticket comes back to you to complete.

## Tips

- Give a specific reason. "Machine down" tells the manager more than "couldn't do it" and speeds up the decision.
- A skip is for *this cycle*. The PM schedule keeps running — the next one will still generate when it's due.
