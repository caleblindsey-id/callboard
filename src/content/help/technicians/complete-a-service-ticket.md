A service ticket is reactive repair work — the customer called with a problem, rather than a scheduled PM. Completing one is simpler than a PM: you log your time, any parts used, notes, photos, and (for field work) a customer signature.

## Step 1 — Open your assigned ticket

1. Tap **Service Tickets** in the menu.
2. Open the ticket assigned to you that's marked **In Progress**.

> If the ticket was created from an estimate the customer already approved, the hours, parts, and notes may be **pre-filled from that estimate**. CallBoard shows an amber reminder — update those fields to reflect the *actual* work you did before marking it complete.

> **Got a PO from the customer?** Enter it in the **Customer PO #** field in the *Customer & Equipment* section and tap **Save**. If the customer requires a PO, that field is flagged **PO REQUIRED** in red — record the number there as soon as you have it.

## Step 2 — Record the work

- **Hours Worked** *(required)* — your labor time on this repair.
- **Parts Used** — add each part with quantity. This is what gets billed, so prices show here.
- **Completion Notes** — describe what you found and what you did.
- **Service Photos** — tap **+ Add Photo** to attach pictures.

### Did extra no-charge labor?

Tap **+ Add ACE Labor**, enter the **ACE Hours** and a **Reason**. This counts toward your payout but does **not** appear on the customer's invoice. It needs manager approval afterward. Skip it if it doesn't apply.

## Step 3 — Signature (field tickets) and submit

- **Outside (field) tickets:** have the customer sign and type their **Printed Name** — both are required.
- **Inside (shop) tickets:** no signature is needed.

Tap **Mark Complete**. CallBoard checks that your hours are valid and — on field tickets — that the signature and name are filled in. As with PMs, any parts that were ordered must be **received** before the ticket can be completed.

## Common questions

- **It won't let me complete.** Check the message at the top — usually a missing signature/name on a field ticket, invalid hours, or a part that hasn't been received yet.
- **The numbers look like the estimate, not what I actually did.** They are — they were copied from the approved estimate. Edit them to match your real hours and parts before submitting.
