When you spot a machine that should be on a PM plan, or aging equipment the customer should replace, submit it as a lead. Approved leads earn you a bonus.

There are two kinds of lead. Both start the same way: tap **My Leads** in the menu, then **Submit lead**, and choose the type.

## PM Lead

For a machine the customer should add to a PM schedule. **The bonus equals the first PM's flat rate** for monthly, bi-monthly, or quarterly schedules, and **half the flat rate** for semi-annual (every six months).

Fill in:

- **Customer** — search by name or account number, or tap **+ New customer (not in system)** and type the company name.
- **Lead contact** — a name, plus at least an email **or** a phone.
- **Equipment** — Make, Model, and Serial number (from the data plate) are required; Location on-site is optional.
- **Proposed start** — the month and year the office should plan to begin the PM cycle.
- **Proposed PM frequency** — optional. **Monthly, Bi-monthly, and Quarterly earn the full bonus. Semi-annual earns half. Annual earns nothing** (CallBoard shows the bonus next to each option). If you're not sure, leave it to the manager.
- **Machine photos** — up to 12, optional but helpful.
- **Notes** — anything the office should know.

Tap **Submit lead**.

## Equipment Sale Lead

For a customer with aging equipment you want replaced. **The bonus pays when we sell a qualifying replacement within 90 days.**

Fill in:

- **Customer** and **Lead contact** — same as above.
- **Equipment tier** — pick the tier that matches the machine. Each tier shows its bonus amount.
- **Machine photos** and **Notes** — describe the make, model, serial, location, and condition.

Tap **Submit lead**.

## What happens after you submit

Your lead shows as **Submitted — awaiting review**. The office reviews it; once approved it's **Pending**, waiting on the customer to buy. When the sale or PM lands it flips to **Earned** with your bonus amount, and to **Paid** once you're paid out.

## Good to know

- The form **auto-saves a draft** while it's open, so you won't lose your typing. Photos aren't saved in the draft, though — re-attach them before submitting.
- "New customer" is fine if they aren't in the system yet; the office links it to the real account when they approve the lead.
